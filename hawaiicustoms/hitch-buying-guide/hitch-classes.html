Hitches

Hitches come in Class I-V. The Hitch is the mechanism that is typically mounted to the rear of a vehicle under the bumper. There are five classes of Hitches for towing trailers. Each class is designed for a specific trailer weight range. Below you will find a basic description of the trailer hitch classes.When choosing the proper hitch for your needs, make sure the weight rating of the hitch exceeds the loaded weight of the trailer you will be towing. Your towing vehicle's capacity for weight carrying and hitch attachment will vary and will may determine the class of hitch that can be used.


CLASS I TRAILER HITCH:  2000 pounds GTW, 200 TW*
            Class I hitches are weight carrying (WC) hitches rated up to 2000 lbs. gross trailer weight (GTW) with a maximum trailer tongue weight (TW) of 200 lbs. The Class I trailer hitch is used to pull any trailer that is 2000 pounds or less.  This trailer hitch is appropriate for most car applications as well as minivans and small SUVs.  A Class I trailer hitch will usually attach to the bumper, truck pan or vehicle frame.  Always consult your owner's manual for vehicle rating. Class I trailer hitches would be used for such things as a utility trailer for hauling jet skis or other sports equipment, a utility trailer hauling a lawnmower or lawn and garden equipment, or a small boat -- basically anything that’s under 2000 pounds, that is a single-axle trailer and does not have tongue weight over 200 pounds. The use of a drawbar and hitch ball of the same rating or higher is required; a higher class drawbar does not increase the towing capacity of the hitch. A Class I hitch usually has a 1-1/4" square receiver opening.
            Inclusions: Our Hidden Hitch brand, Class I trailer hitches come complete with the actual hitch (draw bar), the hitch pin and clip, and a hitch cover/draw bar. The hitch ball itself is not included. 
            Invisi-hitch – Our Class I trailer hitches are also available in Invis-hitch for SOME makes and models of vehicles.  

CLASS II TRAILER HITCH:   Up to 3500 pounds GTW, 300 TW*
            Class II hitches are weight carrying (WC) hitches rated up to 3500 lbs. gross trailer weight (GTW) with a maximum trailer tongue weight (TW) of 300 lbs. Class II trailer hitches are built heavier and hold a bit more weight than a Class I trailer hitch.  These hitches are often used to tow a pop-up camper, a ski boat, a utility trailer and the like.  Appropriate for car, minivan and small SUV applications, a Class II trailer hitch usually attaches to the bumper or vehicle frame. Always consult your owner's manual for vehicle rating. The use of a drawbar and hitch ball of the same rating or higher is required; a higher class drawbar does not increase the towing capacity of the hitch. A Class II hitch usually has a 1-1/4" square receiver opening.
            Inclusions: Featuring single bar construction, our Hidden Hitch brand, Class II trailer hitches come complete with the actual hitch (draw bar), the hitch pin and clip, and a hitch cover/draw bar. The hitch ball itself is not included. 
            Invisi-hitch – Our Hidden Hitch brand Class II trailer hitches are also be available in Invis-hitch for SOME makes and models of vehicles.  

Class III
           Class III hitches are weight carrying (WC) and also are weight distributing (WD) hitches depending on the vehicle and hitch specifications. Not all Class III hitches are rated to be both. The hitch specifications will alert you to a hitch that is not weight distributing. Class III hitches used as weight carrying is rated up to 6000 lbs. gross trailer weight (GTW) with a maximum trailer tongue weight (TW) of 600 lbs. Hitches that are used for weight distributing are rated up to 10,000 lbs. gross trailer weight (GTW) with a maximum trailer tongue weight (TW) of 1000 lbs. The use of a ball mount and hitch ball of the same rating or higher is required. A higher class drawbar does not increase the towing capacity of the hitch. To use this class of hitch for weight distribution requires a weight distribution system. To use the weight distribution side of the hitch a weight distribution system is required. Class III hitches attach to the vehicle frame only. Always consult your owner's manual for vehicle rating. A Class III/IV is appropriate for mini-vans, some full size vans, pick-up trucks and SUVs. 
            Inclusions:  Class III/IV trailer hitches include the hitch itself (also called the drawbar) and the mounting hardware.  These hitches do NOT come with the pin and clip, nor the hitch ball, because each of these trailer hitch accessories are specific to individual applications.
            Invisi-hitch: NOT available for Class III/IV trailer hitches.

Class IV
            Class IV hitches is very similar to Class III hitches except for the weight ratings. Class IV hitches are weight carrying (WC) and weight distributing (WD) hitches depending on the vehicle and hitch specifications. Not all Class IV hitches are rated to be both. The hitch specifications will alert you to a hitch that is not weight distributing. Class IV hitches used as weight carrying is rated up to 10,000 lbs. gross trailer weight (GTW) with a maximum trailer tongue weight (TW) of 1000 lbs. Hitches that are used for weight distributing are rated up to 14,000 lbs. gross trailer weight (GTW) with a maximum trailer tongue weight (TW) of 1400 lbs. The use of a ball mount and hitch ball of the same rating or higher is required for weight carrying. To use the weight distribution side of the hitch a weight distribution system is required. A Class IV hitch usually has a 2" square receiver opening. Class IV hitches attach to the vehicle frame only. Always consult your owner's manual for vehicle rating.

Class V
            Class V hitches is very similar to Class III & IV hitches except for the weight ratings and that the hitch uses a 2-1/2" receiver. Class V hitches are weight carrying (WC) and weight distributing (WD) hitches. Class V hitches used as weight carrying is rated up to 12,000 lbs. gross trailer weight (GTW) with a maximum trailer tongue weight (TW) of 1200 lbs. Hitches that are used for weight distributing are rated up to 17,000 lbs. gross trailer weight (GTW) with a maximum trailer tongue weight (TW) of 1700 lbs. The use of a ball mount and hitch ball of the same rating or higher is required to safely tow these weight loads. To use the weight distribution side of the hitch a weight distribution system is required. Class V hitches attach to the vehicle frame only. Always consult your owner's manual for vehicle rating.
           

Note: Two other hitches to consider are the Fifth Wheel and Gooseneck style hitches. See their sections for further information.
